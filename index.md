# Cultures, Collectives, and Computation

## About

Computational approaches to understanding societies and cultures span disciplines including sociology, psychology, linguistics, education, and computer science. While many Stanford researchers are leaders in computational social science, there is currently no central department or program where trainees can discover related work, exchange ideas, and build connections outside their immediate labs.
The Cultures, Collectives, and Computation (C³) Reading Group was created to serve as a focal point for this interdisciplinary community. Following a “by trainees, for trainees” journal club model, we host a bi-weekly, discussion-based reading group among graduate students, postdocs, research assistants, and other early-career researchers across Stanford. Our sessions provide opportunities to engage with cutting-edge research, present early-stage work, and develop skills in critical engagement and interdisciplinary communication. 

We meet bi-weekly to discuss papers, books, and ideas at the intersection of cultural, collectives, and computation.
